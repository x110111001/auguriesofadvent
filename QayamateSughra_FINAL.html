A Brief Introduction of the Compiler

p.vii

Alhajj Molana Sayyed Mohammad Abbas "Qamar" Zaidi is better known as Doctor Maulana Sayyed Qamar Abbas Zaidi. He was born in a famous Sayyed family of Behra Sadaat, District Muzaffar NagarMuzaffarnagar district · Uttar Pradesh, India (Uttar Pradesh, India). These Sayyeds are well known as Sadaat-e-Bareha. His father Sayyed Amir Abbas Zaidi was a middle-class Zamindar — a respectable man and famous Zakir. His mother belonged to a well-to-do family of Kablawdah, also from Sadaat-e-Bareha.

Maulana Zaidi received primary education in his own village and thereafter got further education in Urdu, Arabic, Persian and English at Mansabiyah Arabic College and Faiz-e-Aam Muslim College, MeerutMeerut · Uttar Pradesh, India (unchanged). In 1947 India was partitioned and, though he had not finished his education, he came to KarachiKarachi · Sindh, Pakistan and joined the Education Department of the Government of Pakistan.

From boyhood he was fond of oratory, which he channelled into Majalis. As a result he is considered a successful Zakir of Imam Husain (A.S.), acknowledged throughout both former East Pakistan and present-day Pakistan.

p.viii

He is also a prolific writer. His books — Asar-e-Qayamat (The Signs of Doomsday), Irfan-e-Imamat (The Recognition of Divine Leadership), Zaid-e-Shaheed (Zaid the Martyr), Qayamat-e-Sughra (Partial Resurrection), Shaheed-e-Salis (The Third Martyr), Miftah-ul-Hajat (Keys to Wish-Fulfilment), Rahbar-e-Hajj wa Ziyarat (Guide to Hajj and Ziyarat), Rahbar-e-Ma Khumaini (Khumaini Our Leader), and Five Weeks in the Iran of Ayatullah Khumaini — are very popular. Being religious-minded since childhood he visited the holy places five times and in 1974 performed Hajj. After the Islamic Revolution of May 1979 he visited Iran as a member of a religious delegation and called upon Ayatullah Khumaini.

He has always been in the forefront of religious movements. To serve his community he established Anjooman-e-Tanzeem-e-Sadaat-e-Bareha (Regd.) and founded Sadaat-e-Bareha Cooperative Housing Society Ltd. He also serves as Finance Secretary of Tanzeem-e-Khairul Amal (Registered), Sadat Colony, Federal 'B' Area, Karachi.

p.ix

He is totally cheerful, sympathetic and amiable — yet courageous, straightforward and outspoken, with an intrinsic dislike of flattery and sycophancy. He practices Homoeopathy in his spare time. For us Mr. Zaidi is worthy of hearty congratulations — despite being a busy man he continues to write as a service to religion. We pray to God through the good offices of the Fourteen Infallibles to accept the labours of Molana and grant continuity to his pen. Ameen.

S. Akhtar Mehmood Rizvi

General Secretary, Maktab-e-Meraj-e-Adab · December 1975

Dedication

p.x

The ship of human destiny is gradually nearing its doom in the perilous whirlpool of Materialism. The so-called scientific progress has increased the greed of man to such an extent that he lusts to gain control over Venus, Moon and Sun — made thereby oblivious to the problems of earth.

The protagonists of culture have degraded humanity so low in the abyss of shamelessness that perhaps the brows of patriarch Adam are wet with sweat of humiliation.

Apparently the world is full of light but dark shadows of ignorance are spreading far and wide. The progeny of Shatan (the Devil) has perfected its design to avenge its dishonour from the progeny of Adam. Horrible circumstances and tumults of crime have closed the doors of human emancipation.

But the only ray of hope in this realm of hopelessness is the light of your self, which shines despite the unseen curtain. I implore you, O Master — manifest yourself and shorten the moments of our longing for you, lest we lose patience. Pray, unfold the covering of invisibility and let your light illumine the world.

Testify these words of the Almighty: "God will perfect His light, though the unbelievers be averse." Your advent is the sign of the arrival of the Day of Resurrection, but your absence is no less painful than Doomsday itself. The suffering humanity needs you. I have perfect faith in your coming and hence — as a good tiding of your manifestation and a form of welcome — I humbly present this book Qayamat-e-Sughra at your holy feet. I hope that you accept me as your humble slave, and that this might be the means of my salvation.

Qamar Zaidi

Opinion

By Molana Sana-ul-Haq Siddiqi, M.A.

p.xii

It is incumbent upon every Muslim that he should believe in the Day of Resurrection. The knowledge of consequences prevents a man from wrong-doing and as such it has been made a tenet of religion. In many verses of the Holy Quran, Resurrection is mentioned explicitly. In the chapter Earthquake, the description is so vivid that even the vibration of the word is sufficient to pass a tremor throughout the body. The belief in Resurrection should not only be a source of fear but a base of character building.

Even if we set aside religious faith, common sense tells us that where there is a beginning there is an end. The great physicist Sir James Jeans in his famous book Mysteries of the Universe discusses this topic from a purely materialistic viewpoint. His conclusion: "All our hopes are ultimately doomed to hopelessness. All our victories and triumphs will end with the end of humanity. The universe will then be as if it did not exist at all."

p.xiii

How Resurrection will occur and in what shape is known only to God. We are given only a few hints — the great earthquake, and so on. It is quite possible that the core of the earth may undergo physical changes of a very high degree resulting in changes on the surface. Scientists project many hypotheses: one such is that the sun is a star of the Nova group — it will expand so much that the earth will merge in its expanse and thus lose its independent identity. Any how, how God will end His universe is His alone to know.

p.xiv

Every great event is followed by certain premonitions. There are therefore portents for this great happening also. Some of the auguries were prophesied by the great Prophet of Islam (S.A.). A number of presages have already taken place. The most conspicuous sign is that the majority of mankind is engrossed in sinful pursuits and, instead of repentance, feels pride in it. Iqbal puts the following words in the mouth of Satan: "The architect is bent upon destroying his own creation."

Moulana Zaidi has bisected Qayamat and named his monograph Qayamat-e-Sughra — that is, small Qayamat or Partial Resurrection — which is prefatory to total Resurrection. Partial Resurrection is thus a revolutionary process. Deeds done in this life will deserve reward or punishment after total Resurrection, and it should be perceived as a conceptual totality. The readers may perhaps differ with some propositions of Maulana Zaidi, but none may differ with his motive — that is, to wean away mankind from evil and turn it towards goodness.

Sana-ul-Haq Siddiqi

Prefatory Note by Hujjatul Islam Talib Jouhari

p.xv

In the name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate. I have before me the matter of the book Qayamat-e-Sughra. In it are compiled the portents of the advent of Imam Mahdi (A.S.) — a matter of unanimity among the Muslims from the beginning of Islam up to this day. Shia writers have done much in this field and their research is worthy of commendation; the efforts of Sunni writers are also noteworthy.

p.xvi

There are plenty of authentic and compact Ahadith. It is essential therefore to present before the public knowledge about the true Mahdi in simple language, so as to close the doors of misguidance. For example, a sect claims that Prophet Jesus and Imam Mahdi are one person. In this matter, if a person knows that Jesus will come to help Imam Mahdi (A.S.) and will offer prayers in his leadership, the matter will solve itself. There is a Hadith: "La Mahdi Illa Masih — There won't be Mahdi without Messiah." However, a large number of Ahadith propound the Messiah to be an Israelite and Mahdi to be a progeny of Mohammad — thus Mahdi and Messiah are two different persons.

p.xix

Collecting particular Ahadith is not a difficult task, but verification is a tough job. Hence it is essential to give particular attention to these matters:

(1) Kufic script passed through several changes before reaching us, formerly devoid of dots and vowel signs. For example, a Hadith may read either "Banners will be burned in the streets of KoofaKufa · Najaf Governorate, Iraq" or "Banners will be torn in the streets of Koofa." Another example: the Hadith about Auf-e-Salmi states his residence is TikritTikrit · Saladin Governorate, Iraq — in Kufic it is possible to read it as his residence is in KuwaitKuwait City · Kuwait. Both places are located in Arabia but certainty is impossible either way.

(2) In narratives, the names of cities and places are given in ancient forms now out of usage. It is necessary to give modern names of the places and cities besides ancient ones to make the Hadith explanatory. (3) Full background of the Hadith should be given — who narrated it, before whom, where — so that references given may be easily recognised.

Talib Jouhari

Prefatory Note by the Author

p.xxi

In the name of God, the merciful, the compassionate. All the intellectuals of the world agree that wisdom is the advisor and guide of man. It guides a man towards goodness and prevents him from evil. It is wisdom that distinguishes man from other animals and makes him the best of creations. The almighty exhorts mankind to use wisdom and think over problems. Even a terse hint is full of details for a wise man. Wisdom without knowledge is useless, because knowledge is the light of wisdom. The almighty says, "You are given but very little knowledge" (17:85). Hence it is sheer ignorance to consider our limited knowledge superior to divine laws.

p.xxii

Thus what is approved in the book of nature and divine wisdom and affirmed by the Holy Prophet's sayings does not become non-existent if we fail to comprehend it. The Holy Prophet declared: "I am the city of knowledge and Ali is its gate." Thus the knowledge of a divine leader is God-given and not acquired through study. His knowledge is unlimited and all-embracing.

p.xxiii

All Muslims believe in the advent of Mahdi, though there are a few differences. Shia Imamites believe that Imam Mahdi is alive and in occultation, as are Prophet Jesus, Ilyas, Khizr etc. Sunnis believe that he is yet to be born and will manifest himself at the age of forty. The greatest proof of the existence of Mahdi is the very pretension of pseudo-Mahdis — copy is made from the original; if there is no original, there can be no copy.

p.xxiv

Eleven years ago I presented my book The Portents of Resurrection and thereafter The Recognition of Imam. The portents mentioned in those books have been almost fulfilled. With changing circumstances I received requests for reprints from every nook and corner, and I decided to present a new book comprising all the essential information plus other portents not included therein. Imam Mahdi won't manifest himself until there take place violent upheavals and changes beyond imagination. For these reasons I name this book Qayamat-e-Sughra, a predecessor to Qayamat itself.

Sayyed Mohammed Abbas Zaidi

Necessity of an Imam

p.1

The existence of the Devil (Iblis) is accepted by all the Muslims, and nearly all other religions. Referring to this unholy soul, the Qur'an warns man that Satan is the sworn enemy of mankind. He wants to misguide total humanity save those who are sincere servants of God. As he is a misguiding soul, there must remain in this world a guiding soul to offset his evil influence. Man is a creature weak in constitution — hence the presence of a misguiding soul and absence of a guiding soul makes his position critical. God, therefore, never leaves the world without a guide. In Qur'an, He says: "O Prophet (S.A.), you are giver of good tidings and a warner and for every community there is a guide." Thus the system of guidance has always been there and will remain forever.

p.2

An Imam is divinely guided and free from any type of sin. Every prophet and Imam is a guide — thus Hadi — and as he is guided by God, he is Mahdi. The final ray of the divine light of guidance is our twelfth Imam. Though he is unseen to our eyes, he sheds "The Light On the Path" while Satan, who is also unseen, continues to misguide people from the Right Path.

The Holy Prophet had said: "There will be twelve chiefs after me, all from Banu Hashim." History is witness that many chiefs came from the Omayyads, Abbasides etc., but none could fulfil the condition of number. Eleven Imams have passed away and as long as there remains the Qur'an in this world, our twelfth holy Imam will also remain.

Imam Mahdi mentioned in books of other religions

Study of various books convinces us that the world is awaiting a superman to save itself from strifes. World religions are unanimous in this regard. Only the names by which the Awaited One is remembered differ. The Abrahamic scrolls name him Master, Psalms name him "Self-Subsistent," Torah names him Augil, the Evangel names him Mohamez, Zoroastrian books name him Khushro, while some heavenly scrolls name him "Word of Truth." Sufis know him as Qutab and Ghaus, Bedouin Arabs remember him as Abu Saleh. The variety of names does not make any difference in the personality of the Awaited One.

p.3

Birth: All the Shias and most of the Sunni erudites agree that he is the son of eleventh Shia Imam Hasan Askari (A.S.). He was born on 15th Shaban 255 A.H. on Friday at the time of dawn. Physique: His colour will be pinkish, thorax and thighs broad, shoulder bones big and muscular. On his back are two freckles — one skin-coloured, another resembling the Holy Prophet's. His secret name is Ahmed; his open name is M.H.M.D. He has round head, beautiful long hair reaching to his shoulders, bright face, broad forehead, thick black beard, slender nose, big bright eyes, and palms pink and fleshy. His physique and behaviour will resemble that of the Holy Prophet.

The major occultation began in 329 A.H. and it still continues.

The Auguries about the Advent of Imam Mahdi

p.7

I have classified the auguries as under. Definite Auguries will certainly appear before the advent of Imam Mahdi and there is no recourse. Conditional Auguries are related to the actions of men — they may or may not appear; repentance and seeking forgiveness may ward off evil omens. Common Auguries pertain to social, economic, cultural and moral changes. Special Auguries pertain to certain communities, particular places, and cities. Past Auguries are those which have occurred before the writing of this book. Future Auguries are those which are yet to take place.

p.8

Another point worth remembering is that geophysical as well as geo-political changes have taken place since that time. Names of cities have changed. Cities have been destroyed. New cities have come into being. Earnest readers should consult Webster's Geography or any other good book about old-time geography.

Common Auguries — The Prophet's Address

p.9

Jabir Ibn Abdullah Ansari is reported to have said: "I performed Hajjatul Wida with the Holy Prophet. After finishing the rituals he said: 'People, today I bid final farewell to the Holy Kaaba.' Then coming to the gate of Kaaba he told people to gather together and began: 'People, may God be merciful to you. You are like the leaves of a thornless tree. This condition would persist for forty years. Then for two years thorns and leaves will live together. After that no leaf would be visible, there will remain only thorns. Listen. I am going to tell you of things to come after me.'"

The Prophet began to weep and the whole crowd also wept with him. After a while the Prophet began: "People, a time will come when: (1) The kings and rulers will be tyrannical. p.10

The Prophet continued: "There will come a time when (1) The belly will be treated as God — people will do anything to fill their bellies without fear of God. (4) Qur'an will be taught as a fashion only. (5) Mosques will be constructed but hearts will lack faith. (6) The scholars will be from amongst the worst people — showing themselves as religious-minded, whereas in fact out-and-out materialists."

"For them the Dirham (money) will symbolize an idol."
